Bald Eagle
The bald eagle is the national bird of the United States, recognizable by its white head and tail feathers contrasted with a dark brown body and wings. It’s a large bird of prey known for its impressive wingspan and strong hunting abilities.
Monarch Butterfly
Known for its striking orange and black wings, the monarch butterfly is famous for its long migratory journey from North America to central Mexico. It’s one of the most well-known butterflies in the world.!
Barn Owl
Recognizable by its heart-shaped face and pale, almost ghostly appearance, the barn owl is an effective nocturnal hunter. It has a distinctive screeching call and is often found in barns or open fields.!
Swallowtail Butterfly
Swallowtails are large butterflies with tail-like extensions on their hindwings, resembling the tail feathers of a swallow. They come in various colors and patterns, often with striking yellow or black markings.
Scarlet Macaw
This large parrot is vibrant with bright red, yellow, and blue feathers. Native to Central and South America, it’s known for its loud calls and strong social bonds within flocks.
Peacock Butterfly
The peacock butterfly is famous for its eye-like patterns on its wings, which resemble peacock feathers. It has vibrant colors, including red and blue, and is commonly found in Europe and parts of Asia.
